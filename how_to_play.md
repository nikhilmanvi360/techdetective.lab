# How to Play Tech Detective: The Digital Crime Lab

Welcome, Detective! You have been granted access to the Cyber Crimes Unit (CCU) Digital Crime Lab terminal. Your mission is to investigate security breaches, analyze digital evidence, and track down the culprits behind sophisticated cyber-attacks.

Here is your comprehensive guide on how to navigate the platform and solve cases.

---

## 1. Getting Started

### The Briefing
You will be competing as a team or individually. When the event starts, you will receive a **Team Name** and **Password** from the Administrator.

### Logging In
1. Navigate to the terminal address provided by your instructor/event admin.
2. You will be greeted by the secure login screen.
3. Enter your assigned **Team Name** and **Password**. 
4. Upon successful authentication, you will be directed to your central **Dashboard**.

---

## 2. The Investigation Dashboard
Your Dashboard is the central hub for all investigations. From here, you can see all active cases assigned to the unit.

- **Active Cases:** Cases marked as "Open" or "Available" can be clicked.
- **Case Difficulty:** Pay attention to the difficulty rating. It's recommended to start with easier cases to get familiar with the system.
- **Progress Tracking:** You can see how many puzzles your team has solved compared to the total puzzles in a case.

---

## 3. Investigating a Case

Once you click on a case, you will enter the **Digital Investigation Board** for that specific incident. This screen is divided into two main sections:

### The Evidence Locker
The left side of your screen contains all the evidence recovered from the crime scene. 
- **Types of Evidence:** You might find raw HTML files, Python scripts, leaked C/C++ code, server error logs, or chat transcripts.
- **Timed Reveals:** Sometimes, evidence won't be available immediately. The system will unlock context sequentially as you solve more puzzles.
- **Interaction:** Click on any piece of evidence to open our interactive code and log viewer. You can switch between different files just like an IDE.

### The Puzzle Interface
The right side of your screen contains the actual investigation questions (puzzles) you need to answer.
- **Dependencies:** Puzzles must be solved logically. Some complex puzzles will be "Locked" until you solve their prerequisite questions.
- **Analysis:** Read the puzzle descriptions carefully. They will guide you on what anomaly to look for in the evidence.

---

## 4. Submitting Flags (Answers)

In cybersecurity, answers to challenges are called "Flags".
1. When you believe you have found the answer (e.g., a vulnerable IP address, a malicious function name, or a leaked password in the logs), enter it into the puzzle's input box.
2. Click **Submit**.
3. **Be careful:** Flags must be exact. If the evidence shows an IP like `192.168.1.55`, enter exactly that.
4. Correct answers will immediately award your team points and potentially unlock the next piece of the puzzle or new evidence!

---

## 5. The "Patrick Jane" AI Consultant

Stuck on a tricky piece of code? You're not alone. The CCU has integrated an advanced AI consultant into your terminal, affectionately codenamed after the famous mentalist.

- **Requesting Assistance:** You can click the "Consult AI" or "Ask Jane" button when viewing evidence or puzzles.
- **How it Works:** The AI will analyze the evidence and your current puzzle, and provide hints without giving you the exact answer. 
- **Hint Penalties:** Be tactical! Depending on the event rules, using hints *might* reduce the total points you earn for that puzzle.

---

## 6. The Scoreboard

Competition is fierce at the CCU. The **Scoreboard** tab allows you to see how your team is performing against rival investigative units in real-time.

- **Real-Time Sync:** As soon as a team answers a question, the scoreboard updates.
- **First Blood:** Keep an eye out—sometimes the first team to solve an entire case gets a bonus!

---

## Summary Checklist for Detectives:
✅ Log in to the terminal.
✅ Select an open case from the Dashboard.
✅ Read the Case Description to understand the context of the cyber-attack.
✅ Open the Available Evidence and investigate the code/logs.
✅ Read the current Unlocked Puzzle.
✅ Cross-reference the evidence to deduce the answer.
✅ Submit the answer to unlock the next stage!

Good luck, Detective. The network is depending on you.
